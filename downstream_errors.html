GTEx Data: Producer Errors vs Consumer Traps

Organised by downstream resource — each tool, database, or package that consumes GTEx data — and split by a strict test for what counts as an error.

Producer error — the value declares A but delivers B: the resource's own output violates what it claims (a mislabeled field, a contaminated measurement, duplicated rows).
Consumer trap — the value is correct and correctly labeled, but easy to misread; the failure appears only when a consumer assumes it means something cleaner than it does.

GTEx eQTL outputs & trans-eQTL / co-expression consumers

GTEx's released eQTL associations and studies built on its trans / co-expression calls.

producer errorliterature-widecross-mapping

Reads from one gene mis-map to a sequence-similar gene, so a distant gene's measured count is contaminated. A real cis-eQTL of the look-alike then surfaces as a fake trans-eQTL: 75.14% of significant trans-eQTLs were cross-mappable, 90.8–97.3% of the top-1000 per tissue (vs 19.1–25.6% background).

Declared “genotype effect on gene B's expression” · delivered genotype effect on B's count = B + reads leaked from gene A.

Saha & Battle, F1000Research 2018; bioRxiv 2025.10.08.681258.

producer errorliterature-widecontamination

~40% of samples carry low-level RNA contamination; pancreas genes (PRSS1, PNLIP…) get apparent eQTLs in tissues that don't express them — 31 of 72 tissues with “inappropriate” eQTLs.

Declared “expression of gene X in tissue Y” · delivered tissue Y + same-sequencing-day contamination.

Halushka et al., Nature Communications 2020.

Consumer traps — correct value, easy to misread

The resource does exactly what it declares. The failure is the consumer's assumption, not the producer's output.

GTEx gene median-TPM file / Portal viz (gtex-viz)

The curated per-tissue median-TPM matrix and its visualization.

consumer trapaggregation semantics

For SELL in Whole Blood the gene value is 1349 TPM while six isoforms sum to 500.96 (2.7×). Both numbers are correct and correctly labeled — gene is a median (RNA-SeQC), transcripts are means (RSEM). The trap is assuming gene = Σ isoforms.

Declared “median”; it is the median. Only the reader's “= sum of isoforms” assumption fails.

broadinstitute/gtex-viz #207.

Reprocessed expression mirrors — Xena/Toil, Recount2, GDC, ARCHS4, MSKCC, Piccolo

Resources that each re-align GTEx and republish gene-level expression.

consumer trapliterature-widenon-comparability

The same samples give discordant TPM across pipelines — 2,068 genes (12.36%) differ >4-fold. Each pipeline's TPM is correct under its own method; they are simply not interchangeable. The trap is treating “the GTEx TPM” as one canonical number.

Each mirror delivers a valid TPM; none declares cross-pipeline equivalence. The error is assuming they match.

Arora et al., Scientific Reports 2020 / UncertaintyRNA; ARCHS4.

Method & transparency

The test. An entry is a producer error only if the value declares one thing and delivers another (mislabeled field, contaminated measurement, duplicated rows). If the value is correct and correctly labeled, it is a consumer trap, not an error — the failure lives in the reader's assumption. Reclassifying the catalogue this way moved gtex-viz #207, pipeline-variability, GEPIA, grex, and PheKnowLator out of “errors” and into “traps”.

Verification. Numbers were quoted from primary sources and reproduce exactly (75.14%; 90.8–97.3% vs 19.1–25.6%; 12.36% / >4-fold; 31 of 72 tissues; 1349 vs 501 TPM; 36,588 vs 36,559 rows; AC/AN swap).
