Downstream Usage Errors in GTEx Data

Automated semantic profiling of the GTEx v8 curated gene–median–TPM matrix surfaces recurring traps: duplicate gene identifiers, a gene symbol that is not a unique key, and a median that is not the sum of its isoforms. This page catalogues real, published downstream errors where consumers of GTEx data hit these and related traps. Every entry was re-verified against its primary source; entries that did not hold up were weakened or removed, and those decisions are shown.

1Literature-wide errors

Errors broad enough to affect many published analyses or a whole class of results.

literature-widecross-mapping

Up to ~75% of standard GTEx trans-eQTLs are alignment artifacts

Exonic reads from one gene mis-map to a sequence-similar gene, manufacturing spurious trans-eQTLs. In GTEx, 75.14% of significant trans-eQTLs were cross-mappable, and 90.8–97.3% of the top-1000 per tissue were cross-mappable versus a 19.1–25.6% background. The GTEx consortium subsequently added a cross-mappability filter to its QTL pipeline; a 2025 analysis shows segmental-duplication mismapping still leaks through.

Source: Saha & Battle, F1000Research 2018 (PMC6305209); bioRxiv 2025.10.08.681258.

literature-widereproducibility

The “GTEx TPM” for a gene is not one number — it depends on the pipeline

The same GTEx and TCGA samples, processed by five standard pipelines (GDC, Xena/Toil, Recount2, MSKCC, Piccolo), give discordant expression: for 2,068 genes (12.36%) the estimates differ by more than four-fold. Within-GTEx comparisons are acceptable after TPM conversion, but the source/pipeline you happen to download materially changes gene-level conclusions.

Source: Arora et al., Scientific Reports 2020; code at sonali-bioc/UncertaintyRNA.

literature-widecontamination

Contamination produces eQTLs in tissues where the gene is not expressed

Low-level RNA contamination affects ~40% of GTEx samples. Pancreas-enriched genes (PRSS1, PNLIP, CELA3A, CLPS…) appear in non-pancreatic samples simply because they were sequenced on the same day as a pancreas sample. Across 72 tissues, 31 carried “inappropriate” eQTLs — a reported eQTL for these genes in brain or blood is a batch artifact, not biology.

Source: Halushka et al., Nature Communications 2020 (PMC7176728).

2Confirmed tool and data-quality bugs

Specific, reproducible defects filed against named tools or resources.

confirmedaggregation semantics

Gene-level median TPM ≠ sum of transcript TPMs (official GTEx tool)

For SELL in Whole Blood, the gene-level value is 1349 TPM while its six isoforms sum to only 500.96 TPM — a 2.7× gap. The gene value is a median (RNA-SeQC) and the transcripts are means (RSEM); the file is not composable from isoforms. Filed publicly against GTEx's own visualization tool.

Source: broadinstitute/gtex-viz #207.

confirmedduplicate identifiers

Pseudoautosomal (_PAR_Y) genes are duplicated

44 genes in the pseudoautosomal region carry a second Ensembl ID (…_PAR_Y) identical to their non-suffixed counterpart. In Signac's GeneActivity() this produced a matrix with 36,588 rows but only 36,559 unique gene names — 29 PAR genes duplicated, fanning out downstream sums and joins. The SuSiE GTEx preprocessing deduplicates by gene ID only, with no chromosome-aware handling, so the duplicates pass through.

Source: timoast/signac #521.

confirmedeQTL data quality

eQTL Catalogue swapped the allele-count and allele-number fields

In release 3 (Oct 2020) the AC and AN fields of the summary-statistics files were accidentally swapped. Analyses that used AN to infer sample size had reduced power to detect colocalizations — a silent data-quality bug in a widely-used GTEx-derived resource.

Source: eQTL Catalogue release notes.

confirmednon-unique key

The gene symbol is not a unique key

A gene symbol maps to many gene IDs (in this matrix, 199 symbols map to multiple Ensembl IDs; e.g. 5_8S_rRNA → 5 IDs). Joining on the symbol silently collides or drops data. The grex mapping package keeps only the first of each duplicate and warns its mapping “may not be 100% accurate”; the PheKnowLator knowledge graph has to deduplicate symbols by hand. Join on the versioned Ensembl ID, never the symbol.

Sources: grex vignette; callahantiff/PheKnowLator.

3Caveated (real but narrower than first stated)

Genuine concerns that were weakened on re-verification — shown here honestly rather than overstated.

weakenedbatch confound

TCGA-tumor vs GTEx-normal comparisons carry a residual batch effect

Tools such as GEPIA compare tumor (TCGA) against normal (GTEx) expression. We initially described this as an uncorrectable confound; in fact GEPIA reprocesses both through UCSC Xena/Toil's uniform pipeline to mitigate it. A residual TCGA-vs-GTEx batch effect remains (confirmed by Arora et al.), so such differential-expression calls still warrant independent validation — but the confound is reduced, not raw.

Source: GEPIA; residual-batch caveat per Arora et al. 2020.

corroboratingreprocessing divergence

Re-aligned GTEx (ARCHS4) differs from other sources

ARCHS4 aligns GTEx with a different pipeline, yielding expression values that differ from other sources. This is the same class of effect as the pipeline-variability finding above; we list it as corroborating rather than as an independent, primary-sourced error.

Source: ARCHS4.

4Method & transparency

How this catalogue was verified. Each entry was checked against its primary source (paper, GitHub issue, or release note) and the specific numbers were quoted from the original, not from a summary. Quantitative claims (75.14%; 90.8–97.3%; 12.36% / >4-fold; 31 of 72 tissues; 1349 vs 501 TPM; 36,588 vs 36,559 rows; AC/AN swap) reproduce exactly.

What was removed. A retracted lung-adenocarcinoma expression-mining paper was investigated as a candidate and excluded: its retraction notice cites systematic publication-process manipulation and peer-review integrity concerns, not a GTEx/TCGA data error.

What was weakened. The GEPIA batch-confound entry was downgraded from “uncorrectable” to “residual” after confirming GEPIA's uniform reprocessing; ARCHS4 was downgraded to corroborating. The shared pattern across every confirmed error: a consumer treated a GTEx identifier or value as cleaner than it is — duplicate IDs, cross-mapping reads, contamination, pipeline-variant values, or a median mistaken for a sum.
